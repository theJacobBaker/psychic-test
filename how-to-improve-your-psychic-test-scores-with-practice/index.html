How to Improve Your Psychic Test Scores With Regular Practice

April 21, 2026 by Dr. Evelyn Moss 3 min read

When people take a psychic test for the first time, they usually want to know one thing: is this real? If the answer is “maybe” or “some areas look interesting,” the natural next question is can I get better at this?

Yes. And the structure for improving psychic test scores looks a lot like training any other perceptual skill – regular, measured practice with honest feedback. Here’s the routine I recommend to anyone serious about developing their abilities.

Build a Daily 15-Minute Drill

Consistency beats intensity. Fifteen minutes a day, six days a week, will outperform an hour-long session once a week every single time.

A good daily drill rotates through three simple exercises:

  1. Five minutes of quiet focus – eyes closed, no media, no distractions. Just notice what arises. This baseline awareness is where all psychic perception begins.
  2. Five minutes of targeted testing – the Zener cards test, a simple coin flip exercise, or a remote viewing session with a partner. Choose one modality per session.
  3. Five minutes of journaling – what you felt, what you got right, what you got wrong, and any patterns you noticed. Be specific about physical sensations, mental impressions, or emotional shifts.

This 5-5-5 structure keeps the session short enough to stick with, varied enough to engage all your faculties, and structured enough to produce real data over time.

Keep a Hit Log

Psychic perception is noisy. Without records, your memory will emphasize the hits and quietly forget the misses – and the result is false confidence that undermines genuine development.

Keep a simple log: date, exercise type, score, and one line of impressions. Over weeks and months, patterns emerge. You might notice you’re sharper in the morning or that certain emotional states improve your scores. You may find you run warm on certain days of the week and cold on others.

This kind of self-knowledge is invaluable. It tells you when to trust your readings and when to rest.

Rotate Between Abilities

Many people fixate on the ability they want to have rather than the one they actually have. If you want to be telepathic but your test results show strong clairvoyance, lean into clairvoyance first. Your strongest channel is your best entry point – and developing it often pulls the others up with it.

Weekly, deliberately rotate your practice across different abilities. Even if one is clearly stronger, the others benefit from cross-training. Think of it as building fluency across multiple sensory languages.

Work With a Partner

Some abilities are almost impossible to test alone. Telepathy, by definition, requires someone to send. Remote viewing is most meaningful when a partner selects and seals targets without your knowledge.

A practice partner – ideally another curious person who’s also developing – doubles the value of your sessions. You can alternate roles and compare scores. You can design games that push both your limits. And honest feedback from someone who knows the target is irreplaceable for calibrating your impressions.

Review Monthly

Once a month, sit with your hit log and retake the psychic test. Compare your results to the baseline you set at the start.

Real improvement looks like this: – Chance is 20%. Your early scores averaged 22-24% – very slightly above chance. – After three months of regular practice, you’re averaging 28-32%, with occasional sessions at 40% or higher. – After a year, your baseline has shifted. Your bad days are where your average used to be.

Progress isn’t linear. You’ll have breakthrough sessions and frustrating plateaus. But the trajectory, measured monthly, should be upward.

The Three Habits That Matter Most

If you take nothing else from this article, remember these three:

  1. Practice daily, not weekly. Consistency compounds.
  2. Track your results honestly. Memory lies; the log doesn’t.
  3. Develop your strongest ability first. It’s the fastest way to feel real progress.
